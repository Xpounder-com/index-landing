05 / FAQ

The quiet questions.

The things that come up on the second call, when the demo is over and procurement is in the room.

How is IDX different from a generic LLM with OCR?+
Every blueprint ships with a typed schema, a validation layer, and a reviewer UI tuned to that document class. We use language models inside the pipeline — for layout, for reasoning over tables, for entity matching — but the output your team sees is structured, auditable data with provenance back to the source page, not a freeform paragraph. Customers consistently report 60–80 fewer reviewer-hours per month per ten thousand pages than they get with generic GPT-4 / Gemini extraction.
Where does our data go? Does IDX train on it?+
No. Customer documents are processed in your dedicated tenant and never enter a shared training corpus. Atelier and Foundry plans support customer-managed encryption keys (CMEK), single-tenant deployments and configurable data-residency in US, EU, UK and APAC regions.
What if our document is weird?+
Most customers pick a stock blueprint and tweak ten or twenty rules. If your document type isn't covered — niche commodity contracts, regulatory filings, your own internal form — the Bespoke track has our solutions team train a custom blueprint on your historical archive with a fixed scope and 30-day delivery.
How do you handle line items and tables?+
Tables are first-class. Every blueprint that ships with line-item extraction also ships with a reviewer view that lets your team correct rows in place, split combined rows, and apply corrections forward to all future documents from that vendor. Line-item accuracy is reported separately from header-field accuracy in your account dashboard.
What does deployment actually look like?+
Day 1 you get a workspace, the blueprint, an inbox alias and an API key. Days 2–5 we sit with your reviewers, watch the first hundred documents go through, and tune validation rules to your domain. Day 6 onward, you're posting to your system of record. The median go-live across our last 50 deployments is 5.4 working days.
What does it cost to get started?+
There's a free workspace with 200 pages of credit — enough to push your top three vendors through and see what the output actually looks like. Studio is self-serve from there. Atelier and Foundry are annual; pricing is volume-tiered with no per-seat fees.
